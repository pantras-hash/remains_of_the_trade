Methodology

What the indices are designed to capture

The paper interprets the post-2018 reorganization of trade through third-country spillovers from U.S.-China decoupling. The site is intentionally data-driven: if the CSV changes, the explorer updates automatically.

01

Export competition

Chinese exports displaced from the U.S. may be redirected to third markets and crowd out other exporters in shared product-destination cells.

02

Import competition

Redirected Chinese goods can increase import penetration in domestic markets, raising competitive pressure on domestic producers.

03

Consumer opportunities

Cheaper Chinese imports may reduce consumer prices and increase access to imported varieties in destination markets.

04

Sourcing opportunities

Cheaper Chinese intermediate inputs can lower costs for downstream producers and potentially strengthen export competitiveness.

How to read ranks

For every selected index, the website ranks countries among those with non-missing values. Rank 1 is the highest value. Missing values are shown as unavailable and excluded from the rank distribution.

The formulas and data construction details are in Section 3 and Appendix A.2/B of the paper. The metadata file data/index_metadata.json controls labels and descriptions shown on this page; the raw numeric values come from data/measures_panel.csv.

Updating the website later

  1. Replace docs/data/measures_panel.csv with the new CSV. Keep the country code column named iso3.
  2. If you add, drop, or rename an index, edit docs/data/index_metadata.json. Missing metadata is allowed; the site will fall back to the column name.
  3. Replace docs/paper/resettling_trade.pdf when the paper changes.
  4. Commit and push to GitHub. GitHub Pages will publish the updated files automatically.
